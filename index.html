Stephen William Hawkings

January 8th 1942 - March 14th 2018

Stephen Hawkings was a theoretical physicist and cosmologist. Notable works include: working on the framework of general relativity and the prediction that black holes emit radation.

Timeline of Stephen Hawkings life:

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England.
  • 1963 - Diagnosed with ALS, a form of Motor Neurone Disease, given two years to live.
  • 1965 - Obtained PhD in applied mathematics and theoretical physics.
  • 1970 - Helped show that time begans in the Big Bang and ends in black holes.
  • 1973 - Published first academic book: 'The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time'.
  • 1974 - Discovered that black holes are not completely black and emit 'Hawking' radiation.
  • 1979 - Eleceted Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge.
  • 1988 - Writes 'A Brief History of Time' which sold over 9 million copies.
  • 2009 - Retireds as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics.
  • 2017 - Awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Imperial College London.
  • 2018 - Dieds at the age of 74 in his home in Cambridge, England.

- Stephen William Hawkings
